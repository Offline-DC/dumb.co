Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Offline DC Inc ("Offline DC," "we," "us"), the maker of the Dumbphone and the software at dumb.co, collects, uses, and protects your information — including the information handled by our Dumb Calendar app when you choose to connect a Google account.

Information we collect

  • Account & device information you provide when you set up a Dumbphone (such as your phone number and device identifiers) so the device and its features can work.
  • Google account data — only if you choose to connect Google Calendar in the Dumb Calendar app. With your consent, the app requests read-only access to your Google calendars and events.

How Dumb Calendar uses Google user data

When you connect your Google account, Dumb Calendar requests the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly scope. We use this access for a single purpose: to read your calendars and events so we can display your schedule on your Dumbphone (an agenda, a month view, and event details). The app is read-only — it does not create, edit, or delete anything in your Google Calendar.

  • Your calendar data is fetched over an encrypted connection and stored locally on your device so your schedule is available offline. It is not uploaded to Offline DC servers.
  • Your Google sign-in tokens are stored in encrypted storage on the device.
  • We do not sell your Google data, use it for advertising, or use it to train any AI/ML models, and we do not share it with third parties.
Limited Use disclosure. Dumb Calendar's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Revoking access & deleting your data

You can disconnect Google Calendar at any time from the Dumb Calendar app (settings → log out). Logging out revokes the app's access token with Google and deletes the calendar data cached on your device. You can also review or revoke access at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

How we protect your information

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including encrypted transport (HTTPS) and encrypted on-device storage for authentication tokens. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to safeguard your data.

Children's privacy

Our products are not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
